What factors go into a decision to launch?

The best time to start a mission is baed on a blen of factors:
the flight's targets and goals, the needs of the spacecraft, the type of rocket, and the desired trajectory,
which refers to the path the vehicle and spacecraft must take to successfully start the mission.

What are the most common causes of scheduling changes in launches?

The most common causes of scheduling delays are related to the weather conditions,
e.g. strong winds can interfere with the rocket's attempt to follow a specific trajectory,
and temperature leaps can cause damage to the rocket's fuel.
